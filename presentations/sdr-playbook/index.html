Playbook

Why targeting matters

Clarity detects identity fraud across the hiring lifecycle.

Three buyer pain points open the door to a conversation:

Identity Fraud

Synthetic identities passing background checks and entering the workforce

Compliance Risk

Regulatory exposure from unverified identities accessing sensitive systems

Insider Threats

Bad actors using stolen credentials to gain persistent access from inside

Playbook

Top 5 Target Industries

#1 Priority

Financial Services

Heavily regulated (SOX, GLBA). Remote fraud directly threatens customer funds and data. High-value targets for nation-state actors.
1,000 – 10,000 employees
#2 Priority

Healthcare

HIPAA mandates identity verification. Patient data is extremely valuable on the dark web. Remote-first trend expanding attack surface.
500 – 5,000 employees
#3 Priority

Technology / SaaS

Fully remote workforces, fast hiring cycles, and engineers with production access from day one. North Korean IT worker schemes target this sector.
500 – 5,000 employees
#4 Priority

Government / Defense

Clearance-required roles and sensitive data. Executive orders now mandate identity verification for contractors and remote workers.
Contractors & system integrators
#5 Priority

Staffing & RPO

High-volume hiring with limited visibility into candidate identity. Fraud scales easily through staffing pipelines. Strong channel partner potential.
All sizes — volume is key

Titles

Security Buyers

Primary persona — they own the risk and the budget

C-Level / VP

CISO

Owns enterprise risk posture. Identity fraud is a board-level issue they need to address proactively.
VP / Senior Director

VP of Information Security

Runs the security org day-to-day. Evaluates and champions new security tools.
Director

Director of Security Operations

Deals with incidents and investigations. Sees the downstream impact of fraudulent hires.
Director / Manager

Head of Insider Threat

Directly responsible for detecting bad actors inside the org. Clarity is their early-warning system.
4
Titles

HR & Talent Buyers

Secondary persona — they own the hiring process

C-Level / VP

Chief People Officer

Responsible for workforce integrity. A fraudulent hire is a CPO's worst nightmare in regulated industries.
VP / Director

VP of People / HR

Oversees all hiring operations. Needs assurance that new hires are who they claim to be.
Director

Head of Talent Acquisition

Runs recruiting pipeline. Directly affected when a fraudulent candidate wastes team time and resources.
Director / Manager

Director of Recruiting Ops

Manages ATS workflows and vendor integrations. Key champion for Clarity's ATS integration story.
5
Titles

IT & Compliance Buyers

Third path in — identity-adjacent roles with budget authority

VP / Director

VP of IT

Manages provisioning and access. A fraudulent hire with admin access is their worst-case scenario.
Director

Director of IAM

Owns identity and access management. Clarity extends identity verification to pre-hire, closing their blind spot.
Director / Manager

Head of Compliance / GRC

Ensures regulatory compliance. Identity fraud creates audit failures and reporting gaps they need to close.
C-Level / VP

CISO (cross-functional)

Often straddles security and IT. When approached from the compliance angle, they become a dual champion.

Common Objections & Responses

"We already do background checks"
Response: Background checks verify credentials, not identity. A synthetic identity with a real SSN and a fabricated work history will pass every time. Clarity verifies the person is real before you check their background.
"We're too small for this"
Response: Smaller companies are actually easier targets — less security infrastructure, fewer checks. One fraudulent hire with admin access at a 200-person company can cause the same damage as at a Fortune 500.
"We use [competitor]"
Response: Great — which part of the hiring lifecycle do they cover? Most tools only check at one point. Clarity verifies identity at application, live interview (deepfake detection), and onboarding. Happy to show where the gaps are.
"Not a priority right now"
Response: Totally understand. Most companies don't prioritize it until after an incident. Would it help to see data on how often this is happening in [their industry]? Happy to send a one-pager — no meeting required.
