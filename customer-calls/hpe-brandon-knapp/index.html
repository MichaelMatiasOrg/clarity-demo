Bottom Line

HPE's insider threat team is actively evaluating vendors for hiring fraud detection; Clarity's Workday integration and end-to-end approach positioned it as a frontrunner, with a follow-up demo and Workday sandbox access as next steps.

Executive Summary

Brandon Knapp, who leads the newly formed insider threat team within HPE's cybersecurity group, engaged with Clarity to evaluate solutions for detecting fraudulent candidates in their hiring process. HPE has identified DPRK-linked and other inauthentic candidates as a recognized risk and has formed a cross-functional project group between cybersecurity and HR/Talent to address it.

The conversation covered Clarity's end-to-end hiring fraud detection capabilities, including CV analysis, deepfake detection, bio-comparison across interviews, and identity verification. Brandon asked detailed, informed questions about Workday integration, data retention, IDV database checks versus visual checks, and recruiter workflow impact. He indicated that HPE is actively evaluating vendors and that Clarity's Workday integration was a differentiator compared to competitors he had already reviewed.

Key Insights

Organizational Context

Insider threat team is new (formed last year). Brandon is evaluating tools on behalf of the cybersecurity org, then presenting recommendations to HR/Talent who hold the budget and will implement.

Current Focus

Exclusively focused on hiring and onboarding right now, not the full employee lifecycle. Taking it "piece by piece." IDV is a confirmed requirement -- they will implement some form of identity verification.

Competitive Landscape

HPE has evaluated id.me, First Advantage (their current background vendor), Encode, and Persona. Key concern with those: lack of ATS integration and standalone workflows that disrupt recruiters.

Timing and Context

Project stalled due to Juniper Networks acquisition (20,000+ employees absorbed in late 2024). Now back on track with project group reformed and vertical leads assigned. No firm timeline but active momentum.

Customer Requirements

RequirementDetails
Workday Integration Native integration with Workday ATS is critical. Previous vendors failed this test. Recruiters must not need to log into a separate platform to see alerts.
IDV Database Checks Brandon specifically asked whether ID checks validate against government databases (not just visual pattern matching). Wants real verification that a license number matches the named individual.
Data Retention Asked about customizable retention periods. Interested in the option to store biometric/facial data either on Clarity's side or in HPE's own infrastructure.
SIEM Integration HPE is migrating to Google SecOps. Need alerts pushed to their SIEM alongside the ATS.
Minimal Recruiter Impact Strong preference for a solution that does not change recruiter workflows. Alerts should surface in-line within Workday, not require additional logins or training.
Customer References HR/Talent team has previously requested customer references when evaluating tools. Brandon acknowledged the sensitivity challenge but will also check his own insider threat network.
Pricing Transparency Needs a pricing estimate to present to HR/Talent budget holders. Wants to understand cost relative to standalone IDV-only solutions.

Product Fit Analysis

1

Workday Integration

Clarity integrates directly with Workday, surfacing alerts inline on candidate profiles. This was a stated differentiator vs. competitors Brandon has already evaluated (Encode, Persona) which required separate platform logins.

2

End-to-End vs. Point Solution

HPE is currently comparing Clarity against IDV-only solutions. Clarity's pitch of CV analysis + deepfake detection + bio-compare + IDV addresses the gap Brandon had not yet considered: that standalone IDV cannot confirm the person who did the identity check is the same person who interviewed.

3

SIEM and Alerting

Clarity confirmed the ability to push alerts to Google SecOps (HPE's incoming SIEM) and messaging platforms like Slack/Teams, aligning with HPE's security operations workflow.

4

Compliance and Privacy

Brandon noted HPE will require privacy and legal documentation. Clarity's GDPR compliance and human-in-the-loop review process for high-risk flags directly addresses AI compliance concerns under emerging AI acts.

5

Flexible Data Retention

Customizable retention with the option for HPE to host biometric data in their own infrastructure maps well to enterprise security and privacy requirements at HPE's scale.
