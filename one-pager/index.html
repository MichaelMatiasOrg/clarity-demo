The Problem: The New Insider Threat

Remote hiring has opened unprecedented opportunities — but also introduced a new class of insider threats. Bad actors exploit AI to penetrate hiring processes with methods nearly impossible to catch with traditional tools:

  • AI-generated CVs & mass applications flood ATS systems with fabricated candidates
  • Interview mules impersonate candidates during technical or live interviews
  • Real-time deepfakes — video avatars and cloned voices — enable convincing impersonation
  • Synthetic identities combine stolen PII with AI-generated documents to create "bulletproof" personas

The stakes: Insider threats cost organizations $11.5M annually on average — through IP theft, espionage, and cyberattack entry points. State-sponsored infiltration campaigns have already targeted Fortune 500 companies.
